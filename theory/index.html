Technology Acceptance Model (TAM)

Why does one user accept a new system while another does not?

yadavari farsi

سودمندی و سهولت استفاده، قصد استفاده را می‌سازند.

یعنی اگر کاربر هم فایده را ببیند و هم سختی زیادی حس نکند، احتمال پذیرش بالا می رود.

Core idea: TAM says people are more likely to use a system when they think it will help their performance and will not be too hard to use. Ease also matters indirectly because easy systems look more useful.

Core constructs: Perceived usefulness, perceived ease of use, intention, use

TAM3

What shapes perceived ease of use before and during implementation?

Unified Theory of Acceptance and Use of Technology (UTAUT)

What broad set of beliefs predicts intention and use across contexts?

Common Comprehensive Exam Questions

These are the cross-theory moves that usually make an answer sound stronger and more complete.

5 prompts

One common comps question asks me to compare initial adoption and post-adoption use. The clean answer is to contrast TAM or UTAUT with ECM. TAM and UTAUT focus on expected usefulness, effort, norms, and facilitating conditions before or around first use. ECM shifts the story to lived experience, confirmation, satisfaction, and continuance. If I want to look stronger in the exam, I should also mention habit from UTAUT2 or Limayem et al. because routine use is not the same thing as first acceptance.

Another common question is about the business value of IT. A strong answer combines RBV, Dynamic Capabilities, and IT Business Value Theory. RBV explains why some IT-related capabilities become strategic. Dynamic capabilities explain how firms renew those capabilities under turbulence. IT Business Value Theory explains the path from IT and complements to process change and then to performance.

A third common question is about the "IT artifact" and how IS can take technology seriously without becoming deterministic. The clean trio here is Affordances, Structuration, and Sociotechnical Systems Theory. Affordances explain what the artifact enables. Structuration explains how people and structures recursively shape use. STS reminds me that the social and technical sides need to be jointly designed.

For dark-side questions, I would usually combine Prospect Theory, Coping Theory, PMT, and Privacy Calculus. Prospect Theory explains resistance and loss aversion. Coping Theory explains stress responses and adaptation. PMT explains protective behavior. Privacy Calculus explains why disclosure still happens even when privacy concerns are high.

For platform and ecosystem questions, I would usually combine Network Effects, TCE, and Platform Ecosystem Theory. Network effects explain tipping and lock-in. TCE explains falling search and coordination costs and changing firm boundaries. Platform Ecosystem Theory explains boundary resources, complementors, and the control-versus-generativity trade-off.
