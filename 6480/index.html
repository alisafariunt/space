BUSI 6480: Experimental Design & Statistical Analysis

PhD Course | Spring 2026 | University of North Texas

Course Overview

This interactive study guide accompanies the BUSI 6480 PhD course focused on Advanced Experimental Design and Statistical Analysis. The course covers ANOVA, mediation analysis, moderation, and related techniques for behavioral sciences research.

Learning Objectives

  • Design and analyze complex experiments using ANOVA
  • Understand and test mediation and moderation effects
  • Master SAS, R, and SPSS for statistical analysis
  • Interpret statistical output correctly
  • Apply experimental design principles to real research
  • Understand assumptions and when to use alternative methods

4-Step Study Method

  1. Read the Study Guide: Each week's guide contains comprehensive summaries. Persian explanations clarify difficult concepts.
  2. Run the Code: Execute each SAS and R program line by line. Understand what each line does.
  3. Complete Homework: Use the homework guides provided. Check your answers against solutions.
  4. Review & Test: Use self-check questions to ensure mastery.

Key Statistical Concepts

  • ANOVA: Analysis of Variance - comparing means across groups
  • Mediation: X affects Y through M (indirect effect)
  • Moderation: Effect of X on Y depends on level of M
  • Bootstrap: Modern method for testing indirect effects
  • Homogeneity of Variance: Equal variances across groups (critical assumption)
