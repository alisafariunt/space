Learning Objectives

By the end of this course, you will be able to:

  1. Understand different theories used in IS research
  2. Know how to plan and execute an IS research project
  3. Learn about important IS papers, especially ones about AI
  4. Be able to read and evaluate other people's research
  5. Write about research problems clearly
  6. Get ready for doing real IS research in your career

What is FT50?

The Financial Times 50 (FT50) is used to rank business schools worldwide. Only ISR and MISQ from IS made this list. Publishing in these journals helps your business school's ranking.

Key Concepts to Know

Term What It Means
Design Science Research that creates new IT artifacts (tools, systems, methods)
Positivist Research that tests theories with data
Interpretive Research that understands meaning through qualitative methods
Structuration Theory about how people and structures mutually shape each other
Affordances What technology lets you do (action possibilities)
Theoretical Tension The problem or contradiction your research addresses
Gap Spotting Finding what's missing in literature (not the best approach)
Assumption Challenging Questioning what everyone takes for granted (better approach)

AI Policy for This Course

You CAN use AI for:

  • Research and finding papers
  • Proofreading (checking for errors)

You CANNOT use AI for:

  • Writing any part of your paper
  • Writing article summaries
  • Anything that will be submitted as your work

If more than 20% is AI-written or has 20% similarity = automatic F!
